About Me

I’m a developer who gets genuinely excited about clean architecture, efficient systems, and well-structured data. Whether it’s building applications or designing data pipelines, I love creating things that are reliable, scalable, and don’t break at 2 a.m. (at least not because of me).

Over the years, I’ve built all kinds of applications—front-end, back-end, the mysterious stuff in between—basically if it runs on the internet, I’ve probably tried to optimize it. I care a lot about writing code that’s clean, efficient, and scalable (because future-me deserves kindness too). I’m organized, methodical, and proactive, which is a fancy way of saying I actually enjoy planning things and using checklists.

Outside of work, I’m a serious coffee enthusiast. I don’t just drink coffee—I optimize it. Grind size, brew time, extraction ratio… yes, I treat it like a data problem. And I deeply admire that magical moment when milk explodes into coffee and creates those beautiful swirling patterns. It’s basically real-time fluid dynamics meets art—and I will absolutely pause to appreciate it. Staying active is also a big part of my life. I play table tennis and tennis, which keeps my reflexes sharp—great for returning serves and debugging production issues. I also love hiking and discovering new trails. There’s something about being in nature that resets the brain… especially after staring at a screen trying to figure out why something “works on my machine.”

In short: I write solid code, drink strong coffee, chase small balls at high speed, and occasionally climb mountains—both literal and technical ones.
